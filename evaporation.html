Method & Formulae

1. Objective

When water is completely evaporated, all dissolved ions and minerals remain behind as solid residue. This calculator estimates the total mass and composition of those solids from laboratory water analysis reported in ppm (mg/L).

2. Unit Definitions

  • ppm = parts per million = mg/L (for dilute aqueous solutions, 1 ppm ≈ 1 mg of solute per litre of water)
  • Volume: 1 m³ = 1,000 L
  • Mass: 1 kg = 1,000 g = 1,000,000 mg

3. Core Formulae

Volume in litres

VL = Vm³ × 1,000

Mass of each dissolved species (kg)

mi (kg) = Ci (ppm) × Vm³ ÷ 1,000

mi (kg) = Ci (mg/L) × VL (L) ÷ 1,000,000

Total solids from reported TDS (kg)

mTDS (kg) = TDS (ppm) × Vm³ ÷ 1,000

Composition by mass (%)

%i = ( mi ÷ Σmions ) × 100

4. Calculation Steps

  1. Convert water volume from m³ to litres: multiply by 1,000.
  2. For each ion (Na⁺, Cl⁻, SO₄²⁻, etc.), multiply its ppm concentration by the volume in m³ and divide by 1,000 to get mass in kg.
  3. Sum all individual ion masses to get Σmions.
  4. Calculate each ion's percentage: divide its mass by the ion sum and multiply by 100.
  5. Separately calculate total solids from the reported TDS value using the same mass formula.
  6. Plot results in pie and bar charts for visual comparison.

5. Worked Example (100 m³, Raw Water Sodium)

Given: Sodium = 200 ppm, Volume = 100 m³

mNa = 200 × 100 ÷ 1,000 = 20 kg

Shortcut: for 100 m³, each 1 ppm contributes exactly 0.1 kg of solid.

Given: TDS = 760 ppm, Volume = 100 m³

mTDS = 760 × 100 ÷ 1,000 = 76 kg total solids

6. Parameters NOT Summed (Avoid Double-Counting)

  • Alkalinity as CaCO₃ — a derived equivalent measure; bicarbonate/carbonate already counted separately.
  • Hardness as CaCO₃ — derived from Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺; those ions are already listed individually.
  • These are shown in the table as reference only.

7. Assumptions & Limitations

  • Water is evaporated to complete dryness — all water removed.
  • Concentrations are assumed uniform throughout the volume.
  • No chemical precipitation or ion-pairing reactions are modelled (actual salt forms may differ, e.g. NaCl, CaSO₄, MgSO₄).
  • BDL (Below Detection Limit) and Nil are treated as zero.
  • The ion sum may differ slightly from reported TDS due to unmeasured species, rounding, and overlapping parameters in the lab report.
