Method & Formulae

Standard: ASTM D-3588 with GPA 2145 component constants

Base conditions: 60 °F (15.56 °C), 14.73 psia, dry gas

Ideal Relative Density

Mole-fraction-weighted sum of GPA 2145 ideal component relative densities (air = 1.0000). C₆+ uses n-hexane average when present.

Formula

RDideal = Σ (xi × Gi)

Ideal Gross Heating Value

Higher heating value with all combustion water condensed. Scaled from 14.696 to 14.73 psia.

Formula

HHVideal = Σ (xi × HHVi) × (14.73 / 14.696)

Units: Btu/scf

Wobbe Index (Superior)

Burner interchangeability — gases with equal Wobbe deliver equal heat at fixed orifice pressure.

Formula

WI = HHVideal / √RDideal

Units: Btu/scf

Real Gas Correction (GPA 2172)

Compressibility at base: Z = Σ (xi × bi). Real values = ideal ÷ Z.

Formula

RDreal = RDideal / Z

HHVreal = HHVideal / Z

Check — pure methane: RD = 0.5539  ·  HHV = 1012.3 Btu/scf  ·  WI = 1360.2 Btu/scf

Accuracy & Limitations

How reliable these results are, and what they do not represent

Bottom line: Ideal relative density, ideal gross heating value, and Wobbe index are calculated correctly per ASTM D-3588 / GPA 2145 and are suitable for engineering comparison across the three Mari Gas samples. They are not a substitute for a certified GC or BTU analyzer for custody transfer or billing disputes.

Sources of Difference vs. a Lab Certificate

  • Composition normalization — Reported totals are 99.83%, 99.76%, and 99.99% mol%. Values are normalized to 100%, which is standard practice. Effect on results: < 0.2%.
  • neo-Pentane — Not tabulated in GPA 2145; constants are from GPA TP-17 (near iso-pentane). At 0.0004–0.0017 mol%, impact is negligible.
  • GPA edition — Constants are from GPA 2145-09. GPA 2145-16 may differ slightly, typically < 0.1 Btu/scf on HHV.
  • Base pressure — Results are at 14.73 psia. ASTM reference is 14.696 psia (+0.23% correction applied). Labs reporting at 14.696 psia will show values ~0.2% lower.
  • Line pressure & temperature — Operating conditions (e.g. 1215 psig, 155 °F) are shown for reference only. Base-condition RD, GHV, and Wobbe depend on composition, not line P or T.
  • Ideal vs. real — Custody transfer often uses real GHV (ideal ÷ Z). For these samples, real GHV is only ~0.1–0.3% higher than ideal (e.g. Raw Gas: 478.5 vs 477.7 Btu/scf).

What This Calculator Is Not

  • Not ISO 6976 (15 °C base) — US ASTM uses 60 °F; values differ by roughly 0.5–1%.
  • Not a rigorous hydrocarbon dew point calculation (no EOS such as GERG-2008 or AGA 8).
  • Not certified for revenue metering or contractual BTU determination without independent verification.
